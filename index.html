Overview

Geo Block is a location-based app and browser blocker: you draw zones on a map, choose which apps or websites are restricted inside them, and the app enforces that automatically. This policy explains what information Geo Block accesses on your device, how it's used, and — most importantly — what never leaves your device.

In short: Geo Block has no servers, no user accounts, no analytics, and no advertising. Everything it needs to function — your zones, your blocklists, your settings — is stored only in the app's private storage on your own device.

What we collect

Geo Block does not collect information in the sense of gathering it off your device. The app reads certain signals from the device it's running on, strictly to operate the blocking rules you've configured:

  • Your device's location, to determine whether you're inside or outside a zone you created.
  • The name of the app currently in the foreground, to check it against your blocklist while you're inside a zone.
  • The list of apps installed on your device, so you can pick which ones to block.

None of this is transmitted to us or to any third party — Geo Block has no backend to send it to. It's read, compared against your local settings, and discarded or written to local storage.

Location data

Location access is the core of how Geo Block works: zones are geofences, and the app needs to know when you cross their boundary — including while the app is closed or the screen is off — to turn blocking on or off at the right moment. This is why Geo Block requests "Allow all the time" location access rather than "only while using the app."

Location is processed on-device only, used solely to evaluate zone entry and exit, and is never logged to a history, never shown to anyone else, and never transmitted anywhere.

Permissions, and why we ask

  • ACCESS_FINE_LOCATION
    ACCESS_COARSE_LOCATION
    ACCESS_BACKGROUND_LOCATION

    Detect entry and exit from the zones you draw, even when the app isn't open.

  • Accessibility Service

    Detect which app is currently open, so a blocked app can be closed while you're inside a restricted zone. Not used to read screen content, keystrokes, or any other accessibility data.

  • QUERY_ALL_PACKAGES

    List the apps installed on your device so you can choose which ones belong on a zone's blocklist.

  • Device Administrator

    Requires Geo Block to be deactivated as a device admin before it can be uninstalled, and allows it to lock the screen — both intended to stop blocking rules from being casually bypassed on a shared or managed device.

  • RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED

    Restart the blocking service after the device reboots, so zones keep working without you having to reopen the app.

  • INTERNET
    ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE

    Open web pages you navigate to from within the app (for example, blocked-site prompts) and check connectivity. Not used to send app data to any server.

Storage & backup

Your zones, blocklists, and settings are stored locally in the app's private storage on your device and are never uploaded by Geo Block. If your device has Android's built-in automatic backup turned on (Settings › System › Backup), that OS-level feature may include this app's data in your Google Account backup, the same way it does for most Android apps — Geo Block does not initiate, access, or process that backup itself.

Uninstalling the app removes its local data from your device.

Sharing & third parties

Geo Block does not share, sell, or transmit your data to anyone, because it has no mechanism to transmit it at all. The app contains no advertising SDKs, no analytics or crash-reporting SDKs, and no third-party trackers.

Children

Geo Block is a utility operated by whoever manages the device it's installed on — typically a parent, guardian, or the device owner themselves — to set blocking rules. It is not directed at children as an audience and does not knowingly collect information from children, since it does not collect information from anyone in the first place.

Your choices

  • Revoke location, or any other permission, at any time in Android's app settings — this will limit or disable the corresponding blocking feature.
  • Turn off the Accessibility Service or Device Administrator access in Android's Accessibility or Device Admin settings.
  • Delete a zone or its blocklist directly within the app.
  • Uninstall the app to remove all locally stored data.
